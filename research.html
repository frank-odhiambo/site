  • Mobile Apps for Inclusive Education

    • Researchers: Frank Otieno Odhiambo, Isabel GÃ¼nther

    This project aims provide rigorous evidence on the impact of technology and e-learning on learners with disabilities, focusing on a mobile app in Kenya that offers curriculum material and motivational messages to special needs students. A field experiment is conducted with primary school learners with disabilities to determine if app usage improves test scores, secondary school transition rates, and mental well-being. In addition, the study analyzes the effectiveness of disability-targeted policies in sub-Saharan Africa by investigating the impact of passing disability laws on the enrollment and years of schooling of special needs learners. With one in seven people worldwide having some form of disability and many facing educational disparities, understanding the role of technology and policy in improving special needs learners' outcomes is crucial.
